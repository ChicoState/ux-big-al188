# The Duolingo Onboarding Experience
The process outlined below steps through the onboarding experience for new users in the famous app Duolingo, which is a language learning platform that has recieved some degree of controversy for it's ability to teach people new languages and addictive design.

![](assets/j01_3.png)

The onboarding experience provides several **affordances** that help new users identify what to click and when, though it falls short in several instances. When a user is on a page that requires a selection, the "Continue" button is grayed out until something is chosen, at which point the selection is highlighted and the button turns green, clearly signaling that the user may advance. This expectation is broken in a few places, most notably the browser notification prompt, which displays a "Continue" button but instantly advances the page upon selecting "Block" or "Allow" without waiting for user input. This means a tentative selection becomes permanent, and adjusting it requires navigating outside the app entirely. The onboarding flow also lacks any backwards navigation meaning once a choice is made there is no way to revisit it. This could lock users into answers they are not satisfied with, or worse, force them to restart the entire onboarding process just to change a single response, which is neither **efficient** nor **satisfying**.

On the other hand, the inclusion of Duo, the brand's owl mascot, is a genuine strength. At each step, Duo provides context for what is being asked, and most questions I had in a given moment were answered or at least addressed by the accompanying dialogue. The quiz also reflects my **mental model** of a typical onboarding experience. Expectations are met with little surprise, and each follow-up step feels familiar from other apps I have used. Where the experience subtly departs from this is the streak system and XP, which attempt to gamify learning by appealing to users' desire to "win," quietly turning what began as curious exploration into an obligation to maintain a streak.
